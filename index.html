Pedagogy & Teaching Standards

How a KCE lesson is taught — every time.

Build the relationship first

Students learn from people they trust. Open with warmth, learn their interests, and connect material to what they care about. A nervous or disengaged student cannot absorb content — your first job is to make the lesson feel safe and personal.

Teach to the individual

Before you teach, know the goal: a grade, a test score, conversational fluency, confidence. Diagnose where the student actually is, then differentiate — adjust pace, examples, and modality (visual, verbal, hands-on) to this learner. Re-teach without judgment when something doesn't land.

The KCE lesson arc

  1. Warm-up & connect — greet, check in, set today's goal.
  2. Teach — clear, scaffolded instruction in small steps.
  3. Practice — student does the work; you coach.
  4. Check for understanding — confirm mastery before moving on.
  5. Wrap & next steps — recap wins, assign light follow-up, preview next lesson.

Inclusive instruction & accommodations

Many KCE students learn differently — including students on Florida's FES-UA (unique-abilities) scholarship, and learners with IEPs, 504 plans, ADHD, dyslexia, anxiety, or autism. Inclusive teaching is core to who we are, not an add-on.

  • Ask and adapt. Ask families about accommodations that help — extended time, chunked tasks, frequent breaks, visual supports, reduced-distraction pacing — and build them into the lesson.
  • Strengths-first. Presume competence, lead with what the student does well, and never talk down to a learner because of a diagnosis.
  • Patience over pace. Progress for some students is measured in confidence and small wins. Honor that — it is exactly the boutique standard.
  • Stay in your lane. You are not diagnosing or providing therapy. If a student needs support beyond instruction, flag it to KCE rather than improvising.

Consistency & communication

Come prepared with materials ready and tech tested. Track each student's progress and share brief, encouraging updates with families. Consistency — same instructor, same standard, reliable follow-through — is the heart of the boutique promise.

Child Safety & Working with Minors

Most KCE students are under 18. Protecting them is non-negotiable and comes before everything else.

Background check (Checkr) is required

Anyone who interacts with students under 18 must clear a Checkr background check before their first lesson. This protects students and is a condition of working with minors at KCE. You'll complete it through a secure link KCE sends you.

Transparency & approved channels only

  • Stay in KCE channels. All contact with a minor happens through KCE-approved platforms (KCE email, Zoom, scheduled lessons). Never contact a student through personal phone, texts, personal email, social media, or direct messages.
  • Keep parents/guardians informed. Families should always be able to see how, when, and where their child is being taught. No secrets with a child, ever.
  • In-person lessons happen only in approved, appropriate settings with the parent/guardian's knowledge and consent — never in a private, isolated setting.
  • Boundaries. No gifts, no private social connections, no romantic or sexualized content of any kind. Maintain a warm but clearly professional relationship.

Mandatory reporting

If you ever suspect a child is being abused, neglected, or is in danger, you are required to act. Report to the Florida Abuse Hotline at 1-800-962-2873 (1-800-96-ABUSE) and notify KCE leadership immediately. When a child's safety is in question, reporting is never optional.

If a student discloses harm or is in crisis during a lesson

If a student tells you they're being hurt, or is in distress or danger in the moment:

  • Stay calm and listen. Take it seriously; don't react with alarm or judgment.
  • Don't promise secrecy. Never say "I won't tell anyone." Gently explain that to keep them safe you may need to tell someone who can help.
  • Don't investigate or interrogate. Don't press for details, lead the child, or try to confirm it yourself — that's the professionals' role.
  • Ensure immediate safety, then report to the Florida Abuse Hotline and notify KCE leadership the same day. If a child is in immediate danger, call 911.
  • Write down what was said in the child's words as soon as possible, and share it only with KCE leadership and authorities.

Anti-Harassment & Anti-Discrimination

KCE maintains a zero-tolerance policy. Everyone — students, families, and colleagues — is treated with dignity and respect.

Equal opportunity

KCE does not tolerate discrimination or harassment based on race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, or any other characteristic protected by law. This applies to every interaction you have while representing KCE.

What sexual harassment is

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal, visual, or physical conduct of a sexual nature. It takes two common forms:

  • Quid pro quo — making any benefit (a referral, a schedule, a grade, continued lessons) conditional on submitting to sexual conduct.
  • Hostile environment — comments, images, jokes, messages, or behavior of a sexual nature that are unwelcome or intimidating.

Prohibited conduct includes sexual comments or "jokes," unwanted advances or messages, sharing sexual content, and any sexualized behavior toward a student, family member, or colleague. Any sexualized conduct involving a minor is absolutely prohibited and will be reported.

How to report — and our promise

If you experience or witness harassment or discrimination, report it immediately to Jonathan King-Cretot (kce@kingcretot.com · 914-809-0112). If the concern involves Jonathan, report to Fabrice King-Cretot (fabrice@kingcretot.com). KCE investigates every report and strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith.

Online & In-Person Teaching Etiquette

The boutique standard shows up in the details — how you present and how you run the room.

Teaching online (Zoom)

  • Join early — be in the room a few minutes before the student.
  • Camera on, framed at eye level, with good lighting and a tidy, neutral, professional background.
  • Quiet space & clear audio — use a headset if needed; no background noise or interruptions.
  • Engage — use screen share, a virtual whiteboard, and interactive prompts; don't lecture at the camera.
  • Recording follows KCE policy and requires parent/guardian consent — never record a minor without it.
  • Dress and conduct yourself as you would in a family's home. End on time and recap next steps.

Teaching in person

  • Meet only in approved, appropriate, non-isolated settings with parent/guardian knowledge and consent.
  • Arrive early, dressed professionally, with materials prepared.
  • Maintain clear professional boundaries and prioritize the student's safety and comfort at all times.

Always have a backup plan

Tech fails. Know how to rejoin a dropped call, have the family's KCE-channel contact handy, and notify KCE if a lesson can't proceed. Calm, prepared problem-solving is part of the boutique experience.
